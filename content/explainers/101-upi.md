# UPI 101: Understanding India's Unified Payments Interface

## What is UPI?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) is India's real-time instant payment system, developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI). It enables round-the-clock fund transfers between bank accounts using a single virtual address or by scanning QR codes.

## Key Components

### Virtual Payment Address (VPA)
Your unique UPI identity in the format `yourname@bankname`

### UPI Apps
- PhonePe
- Paytm
- Google Pay
- BHIM
- Amazon Pay
- WhatsApp Pay

### Transaction Limits
- Per transaction: ₹1,00,000 (varies by bank)
- Daily limit: ₹2,00,000

## How UPI Works

1. **Registration**: Link your bank account to a UPI app
2. **Create VPA**: Generate your unique payment address
3. **Scan or Enter**: Scan QR code or enter recipient's VPA
4. **Authenticate**: Verify with UPI PIN
5. **Instant Transfer**: Money transferred in real-time

## UPI Statistics (2026)

- Active users: 300+ million
- Merchants onboarded: 50+ million
