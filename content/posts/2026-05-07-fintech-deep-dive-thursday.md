# Fintech Deep Dive — Thursday | May 07, 2026

**Focus:** International & Cross-Border Payments — Global fintech, UPI abroad, stablecoins, and cross-border FX
**Coverage Period:** May 1-7, 2026

## Executive Summary

This week's cross-border fintech landscape is defined by explosive growth in stablecoin payments infrastructure, major funding rounds for international money transfer platforms, and strategic partnerships expanding global reach. Key developments include OpenFX's $94M Series A to power cross-border stablecoin payments in Southeast Asia, Ebury's £550M raise as Santander increases its stake to 55%, and stablecoin transaction volumes hitting a record $4.5 trillion in Q1 2026. Meanwhile, legacy payments players like Visa and J.P. Morgan are doubling down on cross-border FX capabilities, while Indian fintechs like KreditBee prepare for international expansion and IPO.

## Key Developments

### 1. OpenFX Raises $94M Series A to Power Cross-Border Stablecoin Payments in Southeast Asia

Miami-based fintech OpenFX has emerged from stealth with a $94 million Series A funding round co-led by Ribbit Capital and existing investors, positioning itself as a critical infrastructure player in the cross-border stablecoin payments ecosystem. The company is building payment infrastructure designed to connect traditional bank accounts to blockchain networks, targeting financial institutions seeking to leverage stablecoins for international transfers.

**Key Details:**
- **Funding:** $94 million Series A (co-led by Ribbit Capital and existing investors)
- **Focus:** Cross-border stablecoin payment infrastructure for financial institutions
- **Expansion:** Southeast Asia priority market
- **Strategic Positioning:** Competes with emerging stablecoin infrastructure players in the B2B payments space

**Market Context:**
OpenFX enters a rapidly evolving landscape where stablecoins are transitioning from crypto-native tools to institutional-grade settlement layers. The company's focus on Southeast Asia is particularly strategic, given the region's growing digital adoption, cross-border trade volumes, and regulatory experimentation with digital assets. The funding will support engineering expansion, Southeast Asia market entry, and infrastructure stack deepening through potential acquisitions.

**Significance:**
This round signals growing institutional confidence in stablecoin-based cross-border payments as a viable alternative to traditional SWIFT and correspondent banking networks. For Indian fintechs operating in global markets, OpenFX's infrastructure could become relevant for integrating stablecoin rails into existing cross-border payment workflows, potentially reducing FX costs and settlement times for international transactions.

### 2. Ebury Raises £550M as Santander Ups Stake to 55% — Cross-Border FX Dominance Accelerates

UK-based cross-border payments platform Ebury has announced a £550 million funding round that will see majority shareholder Santander increase its stake from 50.1% to 55%. The investment, comprising £500 million in equity and £50 million in debt, underscores the strategic importance of cross-border FX infrastructure in the post-pandemic global economy.

**Key Details:**
- **Total Raise:** £550 million (£500M equity + £50M debt)
- **Santander's Stake:** Increases from 50.1% to 55%
- **Previous Investment:** Santander acquired 50.1% stake in April 2020 for £350 million
- **Use of Funds:** Growth capital, technology investment, and expansion
- **Business Model:** SME-focused cross-border FX and payments, serving businesses with international operations

**Market Context:**
Ebury's growth trajectory reflects broader trends in cross-border fintech. The company has built a platform that connects SMEs with access to institutional-grade currency conversion, eliminating the FX spreads and opaque pricing that have traditionally plagued small businesses engaged in international trade. Santander's increasing stake signals confidence in Ebury's business model and its strategic importance to the bank's global expansion plans.

**Significance:**
This deal highlights how traditional banks are acquiring fintech expertise to strengthen their cross-border capabilities. For Indian fintechs, Ebury's model of aggregating liquidity from multiple banks to offer competitive FX rates to SMEs is directly applicable to the Indian market, where small businesses frequently struggle with foreign exchange costs and payment delays in international trade.

### 3. Stablecoin Transaction Volumes Hit Record $4.5 Trillion in Q1 2026

A16z's crypto report has revealed that stablecoin transfer volume reached an unprecedented $4.5 trillion in the first quarter of 2026, representing a structural shift in global payments. This surge comes as Wall Street begins pricing stablecoins as a "structural risk to credit-card networks" that have dominated global payments for half a century.

**Key Details:**
- **Q1 2026 Volume:** $4.5 trillion in stablecoin transfers
- **TRON Dominance:** TRON DAO processed $2 trillion in cumulative $USDT transfers with 98.6% market share
- **Bot Traffic:** Up to 76% of cross-chain volume identified as bot-driven
- **Institutional Adoption:** Banks and multinational corporations increasingly adopting stablecoins for treasury operations and cross-border flows

**Market Context:**
The stablecoin surge is transforming wholesale banking dynamics. Bain & Company has described this as a "great rewiring of wholesale banking," where stablecoins and tokenized deposits move from experimental tools to critical infrastructure underpinning global liquidity. The ability to reduce settlement delays, enable faster capital reuse, and improve capital efficiency is driving institutional adoption.

**Regulatory Landscape:**
The U.S. Senate is advancing the CLARITY Act, with bipartisan negotiators reaching a compromise on stablecoin yield provisions that bars rewards economically equivalent to bank deposits but allows "bona fide activities." The compromise has bolstered confidence in the sector, with Circle stock surging 18% following the agreement.

**Significance:**
For Indian fintechs, the stablecoin trend presents both opportunities and challenges. The technology could enable faster, cheaper cross-border remittances and trade finance, but regulatory clarity remains uncertain. India's stance on cryptocurrencies and stablecoins will be critical in determining whether domestic fintechs can leverage these rails or must partner with international infrastructure providers.

### 4. Visa Canada and RemitBee Partnership Expands Cross-Border Reach to 190+ Countries

Canadian remittance platform RemitBee has announced a strategic partnership with Visa Canada to power instant, secure cross-border transfers to 190+ countries. The collaboration leverages Visa Direct, one of the world's largest digital payment networks, connecting cards, currencies, and markets across the globe.

**Key Details:**
- **Partnership:** RemitBee + Visa Canada
- **Network Reach:** 190+ countries, 150+ currencies, 200 payment systems
- **Coverage:** Reaches up to 4 billion people worldwide
- **Platform Evolution:** Grown into one of Canada's largest cross-border payments platforms over the past decade

**Market Context:**
This partnership exemplifies how traditional payment networks are integrating with specialized fintech platforms to expand global reach. RemitBee's decade-long evolution from a remittance startup to a comprehensive cross-border platform serving both personal and business users demonstrates the maturation of the cross-border payments market.

**Significance:**
For Indian fintechs, this deal underscores the importance of strategic partnerships with global payment networks. Indian remittance platforms like Wise, Remitly, and Western Union have long relied on partnerships with Visa, Mastercard, and other networks to expand their global reach. The RemitBee-Visa partnership highlights how these relationships can accelerate international expansion while maintaining security and compliance standards.

### 5. KreditBee Raises $280M Series E, Prepares for IPO — Indian Cross-Border Lending on the Rise

KreditBee, an online credit solutions provider based in India, has raised $280 million in a Series E funding round co-led by Hornbill Capital and Motilal Oswal. The funding comes as the company prepares for an initial public offering (IPO), signaling growing interest in Indian fintech companies with international expansion ambitions.

**Key Details:**
- **Funding:** $280 million Series E
- **Lead Investors:** Hornbill Capital, Motilal Oswal
- **Purpose:** IPO preparation and international expansion
- **Business Model:** Online credit solutions for consumers and small businesses

**Market Context:**
KreditBee's funding round reflects broader trends in Indian fintech, where companies are increasingly seeking international expansion opportunities. The company's preparation for IPO comes amid a wave of Indian fintech listings, including Paytm, Nykaa, and One97 Communications, demonstrating investor appetite for well-capitalized fintech platforms with scalable business models.

**Significance:**
KreditBee's trajectory highlights the potential for Indian fintechs to succeed in global markets. The company's focus on online credit solutions with potential applications in emerging markets with underbanked populations presents opportunities for international expansion. For DPI-watchers tracking India's digital public infrastructure, KreditBee's growth story illustrates how domestic fintech success can translate to global impact.

## Emerging Trends

### Stablecoins as Institutional Infrastructure

The $4.5 trillion Q1 stablecoin volume demonstrates that stablecoins have evolved beyond crypto-native use cases to become critical infrastructure for institutional payments. Banks and multinational corporations are increasingly adopting stablecoins for treasury operations, cross-border settlements, and liquidity management, viewing them as a way to reduce settlement times and improve capital efficiency.

### Strategic Bank-Fintech Consolidation

Ebury's growth and Santander's increasing stake exemplify how traditional banks are acquiring fintech expertise to strengthen cross-border capabilities. This consolidation trend is likely to accelerate as banks seek to compete with specialized fintech players in areas like cross-border FX, trade finance, and SME lending.

### Southeast Asia as Cross-Border Payments Hotspot

OpenFX's focus on Southeast Asia highlights the region's growing importance in global fintech. Countries like Singapore, Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines are experiencing rapid digital adoption, growing cross-border trade volumes, and regulatory experimentation with digital assets, making them attractive markets for cross-border payments infrastructure.

### Regulatory Clarity Driving Institutional Adoption

The U.S. Senate's progress on the CLARITY Act and Bank of Canada's timeline for stablecoin regulations demonstrate how regulatory clarity is enabling institutional adoption. As jurisdictions develop clear frameworks for stablecoins and digital assets, traditional financial institutions are more confident in integrating these technologies into their operations.

## Implications for Indian Fintechs

### Opportunity: Stablecoin-Based Cross-Border Payments

Indian fintechs can leverage stablecoin infrastructure like OpenFX to offer faster, cheaper cross-border remittances and trade finance. The technology could reduce FX costs and settlement times, particularly for SMEs engaged in international trade. However, regulatory clarity will be critical before widespread adoption.

### Opportunity: Strategic Partnerships with Global Networks

The RemitBee-Visa partnership model demonstrates the value of integrating with established payment networks. Indian fintechs should consider partnerships with Visa, Mastercard, and other networks to expand global reach while maintaining compliance and security standards.

### Challenge: Regulatory Uncertainty

India's stance on cryptocurrencies and stablecoins remains uncertain. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has historically been cautious about digital assets, and any regulatory framework that restricts stablecoin usage could limit Indian fintechs' ability to leverage these technologies. Domestic fintechs may need to partner with international infrastructure providers that operate in more permissive jurisdictions.

### Opportunity: International Expansion for Indian Lenders

KreditBee's IPO preparation and international expansion ambitions highlight the potential for Indian fintechs to succeed in global markets. Companies with scalable, technology-driven business models can leverage India's fintech expertise to serve underbanked populations in emerging markets.
