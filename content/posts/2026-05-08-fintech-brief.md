# Fintech Brief — May 08, 2026

## Today's Top Stories

### Skyroot Aerospace Becomes India's First Space Tech Unicorn

Hyderabad-based launch startup Skyroot Aerospace has crossed unicorn territory with a fresh funding round that values the company at $1.1 billion. Skyroot raised $60 million co-led by Sherpalo Ventures and GIC, with participation from BlackRock-affiliated funds and other investors. The investment comes as Skyroot prepares for the maiden orbital launch of its Vikram-1 rocket, targeting June 2026 from Sriharikota.

This milestone marks India's first space technology company to reach $1 billion+ valuation, a significant achievement for the country's still-nascent private space sector that began liberalising in 2020[^1]. Skyroot's Vikram-1 will be India's first private orbital rocket, following the company's November 2022 sub-orbital demonstration with Vikram-S.

### RBI Rupee Weakening: Structural Pressures Ahead

India's rupee has weakened significantly in 2026, prompting concerns about foreign capital flows. MUFG analyst Michael Wan warns that RBI intervention and tighter monetary policy alone may not be sufficient to support the currency, as weak foreign inflows and slowing FDI remain key structural pressures[^2]. Wan emphasizes that sustained dollar inflows will be needed to stabilize the rupee.

The currency's decline comes amid broader economic challenges, with exporters gaining a 6% edge over competing markets due to the weaker rupee, according to RBI reference data[^3].

### Fairfax India Increases Stake in IIFL Capital

Canada-based Fairfax Financial Holdings-backed Fairfax India is increasing its stake in India's IIFL Capital Services to 51% through an investment of 20 billion rupees ($211 million). The move strengthens Fairfax's control over the diversified financial services company, which operates across wealth management, brokerage, and asset management[^4].

IIFL Capital has been expanding its footprint across India's growing financial services sector, and the increased stake from Fairfax signals continued confidence in India's domestic financial markets.

## Key Takeaways

- **Space tech breakthrough**: Skyroot's unicorn status highlights India's growing capabilities in emerging frontier technologies
- **Currency headwinds**: RBI faces structural challenges in managing rupee volatility amid global capital flow pressures
- **M&A activity**: Foreign investors continue to accumulate stakes in Indian financial services firms
- **Funding focus**: Investors are backing high-growth, infrastructure-heavy sectors like space tech
