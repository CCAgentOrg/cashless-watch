# Fintech Brief — June 04, 2026

## RBI MPC Deliberations Begin; Rate Hike Looms Over Currency Pressure

The RBI's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) began its three-day deliberations on June 3, with the policy decision due Friday, June 6. While a majority of economists polled by CNBC expect the repo rate to hold at 5.25% — marking a third consecutive pause — there is growing chatter that the central bank may defy consensus and hike rates to defend the rupee. India is facing a dual risk: a weakening currency and elevated inflation, compounded by foreign institutional investor outflows that crossed $26.4 billion year-to-date in 2026. Bernstein's Venugopal Garre argued on CNBC that a rate hike would be "more logical" for India, drawing parallels with Indonesia's surprise 50 bps hike on May 20. Separately, Reuters reported that the RBI has sent at least 10 queries to firms and family offices over the past three weeks to scrutinise overseas investments routed through opaque structures — a rare enforcement sweep targeting potential misuse of the Overseas Direct Investment route for private wealth management. ## MUFG Plans $250 Million Fund for Indian Fintech Startups

Japan's Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group (MUFG) is preparing to launch a $250 million (₹2,300–2,400 crore) fund targeting Indian fintech startups, per an Economic Times report. The vehicle will focus on early-stage companies in digital finance, expanding MUFG's India strategy beyond its existing $300 million Ganesha Fund (2022) that targeted growth-stage deals. The corpus may eventually be scaled to $400 million. This comes at a time when overall PE/VC investment in India fell ~17% year-on-year to $36 billion in 2025 (Bain & Company), and fintech deal counts dropped 54% in Q1 2026 even as total funding held steady at $513 million — signalling a clear shift toward fewer but larger, more selective bets. For India's fintech ecosystem, a dedicated $250 million war chest from the world's largest bank by assets is a strong vote of confidence. [^3]

## Bengaluru Fintech Oolka Raises $14M Series A Led by Accel

Oolka, a Bengaluru-based fintech startup building AI tools for credit and personal finance, has raised $14 million in a Series A round led by Accel, with participation from Lightspeed and Z47. Personal investments from Meesho founders Vidit Aatrey and Sanjeev Barnwal also came in. The round takes Oolka's total funding to approximately $21 million. The startup plans to expand its product and engineering teams, deepen AI capabilities, and build partnerships with banks and NBFCs. Oolka sits at the intersection of two hot themes — AI-native credit underwriting and consumer financial health — a space that continues to attract top-tier VC attention despite the broader funding slowdown. [^4]

## UPI Recipient Bank Name Verification Goes Live; RBI Weekly Credit Reporting Deadline Approaches

Two significant UPI ecosystem updates are in play. First, UPI payments now display the recipient's verified bank name before confirming a transaction — a consumer protection measure aimed at reducing wrong-transfer fraud, rolled out as part of the June 2026 changes. Second, the RBI's weekly credit reporting mandate is approaching its next milestone: by July 2026, all credit institutions (banks, NBFCs, fintech lenders) must submit incremental credit data to Credit Information Companies four times a month — on the 9th, 16th, 23rd, and last calendar day. This is a significant escalation from the fortnightly reporting introduced in January 2025, and represents a massive operational challenge for lenders still running on legacy batch-processing infrastructure. Fintech lenders that have invested in real-time data pipelines will have a material advantage. [^5] [^6]
