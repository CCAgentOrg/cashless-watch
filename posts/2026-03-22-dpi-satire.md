## 📜 The Chronicles of Consent Theatre

### Act I: The Aadhaar Pre-install Saga

**Then (January 2026):** UIDAI secretly asks Apple, Samsung, and Google to pre-install the Aadhaar app on all new devices sold in India. The request is described as "routine." [^1]

**Now (2026, looking back):** Every new phone ships with Aadhaar pre-loaded, sitting right next to your clock app. It's as if the government said, "Why wait for you to download it when we can ensure it's there before you've even made your first call?"

As one tech executive reportedly whined (in completely unverified reports): *"First they wanted us to pre-install Sanchar Saathi. Now Aadhaar. Next they'll want us to pre-install their surveillance appreciation app."*

The government called this "convenience." Privacy experts called it something else entirely. The Supreme Court, in a ruling that surprised absolutely no one, noted that "voluntary" programs becoming de facto mandatory is just "how technology works sometimes."

---

### Act II: ONDC — The Network That Loved Nobody

Remember when ONDC was supposed to revolutionize Indian commerce? The promise was beautiful: an open network where small sellers could compete with Amazon, where consumers would have choices beyond the two major platforms.

**Reality (2026):** ONDC processes approximately 50-100 transactions daily across its entire network. [^2] The protocol experiences "severe growing pains" because of what experts call "a fundamental market misunderstanding: ONDC isn't an app. It's a protocol."

But who wants to explain protocols to shareholders?

Meanwhile, the government celebrates ONDC's expansion while economists quietly recalculate GDP contributions. One analyst noted: "It's like building a highway and being proud of the toll booths without checking if anyone actually drives on it."

**Featured Testimonial:**
> "I signed up for ONDC as a seller. Then I signed up again. Then I got a confirmation. Then nothing happened for six weeks. Then I forgot I signed up. Then my phone died. Then I moved on with my life."
> — *Priya Sharma, Mumbai D2C Entrepreneur*

### Act III: The Face Recognition Revolution

In 2026, face recognition has become so ubiquitous that looking at your phone is now a form of government interaction. Smart cities deploy cameras at every junction. The police use facial verification during routine checks. Your face is now your ID, whether you consented or not.

**The Convenience:** No need to carry ID! Just let any random camera scan your face and... oops, there's a notification that you're "matched" to a warrant from 2019.

**The Privacy:** The Supreme Court has noted that surveillance orders often use vague phrases like "reasons of public safety" without "factual foundation or demonstrable urgency." [^3] But who's counting?

**As seen in The Consent Theatre:**
- "Your face is just 200 pixels of data. What's the big deal?"
- "If you have nothing to hide, you have nothing to fear."
- "It's for your own safety. Now please smile for the camera."

### Act IV: Digital Health Records — Sharing is Caring (Whether You Want To or Not)

The Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) promises unified health records accessible across providers. Your ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account) is supposed to give you control over your medical data.

**The Reality:** The "consent manager" architecture requires explicit consent for data sharing. But in practice, most healthcare providers have "generic consent" forms that basically say "sign here or we won't treat you." [^4]

One doctor explained: "The consent is technically there. It's just that patients don't read the 47-page document, and we don't explain that their psychiatric history is now available to their employer."

**Featured Testimonial:**
> "I went to a dentist for a toothache. Now my insurance company knows I had a cavity filled AND that I've been taking anxiety medication for stress. Thanks, ABDM!"
> — *Anonymous, Bengaluru*

## 📊 The Factual Matrix

**Aadhaar Pre-install Push (January 2026):**
- UIDAI requested Apple, Samsung, Google to pre-install Aadhaar app on all new devices [^1]
- Already required Sanchar Saathi app to be pre-loaded (November 2025)
- Industry body ITI opposed the move, citing security and sovereignty concerns

**ONDC Adoption Crisis (2026):**
- Network processes ~50-100 transactions daily [^2]
- Protocol vs. app confusion limiting adoption
- No meaningful competition with established e-commerce platforms

**Surveillance Without Safeguards:**
- Smart city cameras proliferate without privacy regulations [^3]
- Face recognition used by police without legal framework
- Surveillance orders use vague justifications

**Digital Health Consent Issues:**
- ABDM consent architecture theoretically sound but practically weak [^4]
- Generic consent forms negate individual control
- No penalty for unauthorized data sharing

## 🎯 Your Rights: The Actionable Guide

### What You Can Do

1. **Opt Out Where Possible:**
   - Don't link ABHA to every hospital system
   - Use offline verification when offered
   - Request data deletion under DPDP Act

2. **Exercise Your DPDP Rights (2026):**
   - Right to confirm if your data is being processed
   - Right to correction of inaccurate data
   - Right to deletion ("right to be forgotten")
   - Report violations: grievances@uidai.gov.in, privacy@nha.gov.in

3. **Document Everything:**
   - Screenshot consent forms before signing
   - Keep copies of all data sharing agreements
   - Record dates of complaints filed

4. **Demand Answers:**
   - Ask hospitals exactly who sees your ABHA data
   - Question why face scans are required for services
   - File RTI requests about surveillance deployments

5. **Support Advocacy:**
   - Support organizations challenging DPI overreach
   - Share stories of digital exclusion
   - Vote for candidates who prioritize digital rights
